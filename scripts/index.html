The Challenge

Media operations are still
stitched together by hand.

⛓️

Fragmented toolchains

Dozens of disconnected systems for planning, production, QC, editing, compliance, scheduling, and delivery. Each demands manual orchestration and constant oversight.

🕐

Linear, manual handoffs

Content moves from team to team in sequence — production to post to compliance to distribution. Every handoff introduces delay, errors, and context loss across the entire chain.

📈

Scale breaks everything

Managing 10 channels is operational. Managing 100 across territories, languages, formats, and platforms demands a fundamentally different architecture — one that thinks, not just routes.

The media industry doesn't need another point solution. It needs an intelligent layer that understands the entire chain — from onset to audience — and can act on it.

How It Works

Agents that reason,
not just execute.

Every workflow runs through an agentic loop — AI that thinks before it acts, observes the result, and adapts. Humans stay in the loop where judgment matters.

01

Describe the workflow

Define what you need — in plain language or structured templates. The orchestrator decomposes it into tasks and assigns specialist agents in the right order across the full pipeline.

02

Agents reason & act

Each agent uses domain-specific models and tools. They observe results, detect anomalies, retry intelligently, and escalate when confidence drops. Not scripts — judgment.

03

Humans approve & refine

Flagged items surface for human review at natural checkpoints. Approve, correct, or override. Every human decision feeds back — agents learn your standards over time.
